# Mobile App Decision: Research Synthesis
**Date:** October 2024
**Sources:** User Interviews (5), Survey (200 responses), Support Tickets (10), Sales Notes
**Prepared by:** TaskFlow Product Team

---

## Executive Summary

**Recommendation: Build the mobile app. This is not a nice-to-have — it is an active churn driver and deal blocker.**

Every data source points to the same conclusion. Mobile demand is universal, urgent, and costing TaskFlow real revenue today.

---

| Source | Mobile Demand Signal |

| **User Interviews** | 5/5 users raised mobile as a primary pain point |

| **Support Tickets** | 10/10 tickets in batch were mobile-related |
| **Sales Notes** | $466K ARR lost; $515K ARR at risk; $847K total exposure |

**Primary deals lost to:** Asana (4 deals), Monday.com (3 deals), Linear (2 deals), Procore (1 deal)

---

## Key Pain Points (All Sources Consistent)

1. **No native app** — the baseline objection; heard in 80%+ of mobile-heavy deals
2. **Slow mobile web** — avg 1.9/5 satisfaction; 10–30 second load times reported
3. **No offline support** — critical for construction, healthcare, logistics, events
4. **No push notifications** — users miss deadlines; learn about comments via Slack workarounds
5. **No MDM/enterprise security** — blocking 40–70 seat enterprise rollouts
6. **Mobile workforce exclusion** — field workers have abandoned TaskFlow entirely in some accounts

---

## Who Needs Mobile Most

| Persona | Urgency | Context |
|---------|---------|---------|
| CEOs / Founders | Critical (100%) | Run their company from their phone |
| Operations Managers | Critical (100%) | On their feet all day; desk = 2 hrs/day |
| Project Managers | Critical (68%) | Client travel, site visits, events |
| Engineering Managers | High (90%) | On-call incidents, standups on the go |
| Product Managers | High (74%) | Flights, customer travel |
| Designers | Medium (93%) | Primarily desktop workers |

**Industries with most acute need:** Healthcare, Construction, Logistics, Retail, Events

---

## Competitive Pressure

- **Linear** cited by name in interviews, survey, and support tickets as having a superior mobile experience
- **Asana** and **Monday.com** winning deals directly because of native mobile apps
- TaskFlow's mobile web was "laughed at" in at least one live demo (NexGen Events)
- 81.5% of survey respondents would pay more for a quality mobile app

---

## Minimum Viable Mobile Experience (User-Defined)

Based on consistent feedback across all 4 data sources:

1. View tasks and project boards
2. Update task status
3. Read and reply to comments
4. Push notifications
5. (High value) Offline mode
6. (Enterprise) MDM support / biometric lock

---

## Recommendation

**Build the mobile app as a Q1 2025 priority.**

- **Immediate action:** Communicate mobile app roadmap to at-risk accounts (Atlas Manufacturing $240K renewal deadline Dec 1)
- **MVP scope:** Core task management + push notifications (offline = v1.1)
- **Win-back play:** Reach out to 3 lost prospects who said they'd return ($287K ARR opportunity)
- **Enterprise unlock:** MDM support + biometric lock unblocks stalled enterprise deals
