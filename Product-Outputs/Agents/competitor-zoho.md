# Competitor Brief: Zoho Projects

**Last Updated:** March 2026
**Analyst:** TaskFlow Product Team

---

## Overview

Zoho Projects is a cloud-based project management tool from Zoho Corporation, part of its broader 50+ app business suite. It targets small to mid-sized businesses seeking affordable, feature-rich project management tightly integrated with other Zoho products.

---

## Key Features

- Task management with subtasks, dependencies, and priorities
- Gantt charts for project timelines and progress tracking
- Time tracking and timesheets with billing integration (Zoho Books/Invoice)
- Blueprint workflow automation (no-code)
- Resource utilization and workload management
- Client portal for external stakeholder access (no full user account required)
- Multiple project views: List, Board, Gantt, Calendar
- Native integration with the full Zoho ecosystem (CRM, Desk, Analytics, etc.)
- Mobile apps (iOS and Android)

---

| Plan | Price | Notes |

---

## Positioning / Target Market

Zoho Projects targets small to mid-sized businesses (10–500 employees) that are already in or considering the Zoho ecosystem. It positions itself as a comprehensive, affordable alternative to pricier tools like Asana and Monday.com. Particularly strong appeal for teams that also need CRM, finance, or helpdesk — all within one vendor.

---

## Strengths

- Extremely competitive pricing — significantly cheaper than Asana, Monday.com, and Wrike
- Deep native integration across 50+ Zoho apps (CRM, Books, Desk, Analytics)
- Client portal is a differentiator at this price point — rare in the SMB tier
- Blueprint automation provides enterprise-level workflow capability at low cost
- Resource utilization tracking included at a fraction of competitors' costs
- Established vendor with global customer base and long-term stability

---

## Weaknesses

- Gantt chart functionality is limited compared to dedicated tools
- Cluttered, busy interface with steep learning curve for new users
- Customization options are more restricted than competitors
- Mobile apps are limited to viewing and simple updates — not suited for heavy use
- Notification overload reported by users
- Less appealing to teams outside the Zoho ecosystem (weak standalone value proposition)
- Brand perception is "budget tool" — not aspirational for modern tech teams

---

## Opportunities for TaskFlow

- **UX differentiation:** Zoho's interface complexity and notification overload are recurring complaints — TaskFlow can win on clean, intuitive design
- **Modern team appeal:** Zoho skews toward traditional SMBs; TaskFlow can own the modern, async-remote team narrative that Zoho doesn't serve well

- **Onboarding speed:** Zoho's learning curve creates churn risk at the free/trial stage — TaskFlow's fast onboarding can convert those frustrated users
